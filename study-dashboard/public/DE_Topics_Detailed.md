### UNIT-I: Digital Inclusion & Empowerment

#### 1. Vision of Digital India
The Digital India programme, launched in 2015, envisions transforming India into a digitally empowered society and knowledge economy through three pillars:
- **Digital Infrastructure as a Core Utility:** High-speed internet to every citizen, unique digital identity (Aadhaar), and mobile phones as the primary device.
- **Governance & Services on Demand:** Seamless delivery of government services in real-time from online and mobile platforms.
- **Digital Empowerment of Citizens:** Universal digital literacy and access to digital resources in local languages.

#### 2. Nine Pillars of Digital India
1. Broadband Highways
2. Universal Access to Mobile Connectivity
3. Public Internet Access Programme
4. e-Governance – Reforming Governance through Technology
5. e-Kranti – Electronic Delivery of Services
6. Information for All
7. Electronics Manufacturing
8. IT for Jobs
9. Early Harvest Programmes

#### 3. Key Digital India Initiatives
- **DigiLocker:** A digital wallet for storing authentic government-issued documents (Aadhaar, Marksheets, Driving License). Documents are legally valid under the IT Act. Benefits: no risk of physical loss, eco-friendly, paperless governance.
- **BHIM (Bharat Interface for Money):** Developed by NPCI, uses UPI for 24/7 instant money transfers via mobile number or Virtual Payment Address (VPA). Key tool for financial inclusion.
- **e-Pathshala:** Free multilingual platform providing NCERT e-textbooks (Class 1-12) for students, teachers, and parents.
- **e-Hospital / ORS:** Online Registration System for hospitals—appointment booking, access to digital health records, billing.
- **e-Kranti:** Electronic delivery of services across education (e-Education), healthcare (e-Healthcare), and judiciary (e-Courts).
- **DIKSHA:** Digital Infrastructure for Knowledge Sharing—national platform for teacher training and student learning.

#### 4. Digital Inclusion
*   **Definition:** Ensuring all citizens, regardless of location, age, or income, have access to affordable ICT tools and the internet.
*   **Need:** Bridging the rural-urban digital divide for education, healthcare, and economic opportunity.
*   **Challenges:** High infrastructure cost, digital literacy gaps, English-language dominance, and affordability.
*   **Impact:** Strengthens democracy, enables transparent e-governance, and creates economic parity.

#### 5. Electronic Communication & Collaborative Tools
- **Email (Asynchronous):** Digital letter exchanged across the internet. Not real-time.
- **Blogs:** Dynamic online journals for sharing ideas and information.
- **Social Media:** Real-time platforms for interaction (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram).
- **Collaborative Platforms:** Google Workspace (simultaneous editing), Slack (team messaging), Microsoft Teams, Trello (task management).
- **Video Conferencing:** Zoom, Google Meet—enables face-to-face interaction across geographical boundaries.
- **Online Learning:** Platforms like Coursera, Khan Academy, DIKSHA provide self-paced, interactive education.

### UNIT-II: Safe Cyberspace, Ethics & Emerging Technology

#### 6. Cybersecurity Threats
- **Phishing:** Fraudulent emails/messages pretending to be from banks to steal login credentials.
- **Malware:** Malicious software — includes Viruses (self-replicating), Worms (spread via networks), and Trojans (disguised as legitimate apps).
- **Ransomware:** Encrypts user data and demands payment for decryption. Example: WannaCry (2017).
- **DoS/DDoS (Denial of Service):** Flooding a network/server with traffic to crash it and make it unavailable.
- **Cyberbullying:** Online harassment using digital tools. Prevention: block, report, and preserve evidence.

#### 7. Cyber Hygiene Best Practices
- Use **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** / Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
- Use **strong, unique passwords** and a password manager.
- Keep **software and OS updated** regularly.
- Use a **VPN** on public networks (encrypts data, ensures privacy).
- Verify senders before clicking links or attachments.

#### 8. Government Security Initiatives
- **CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team):** National agency handling cyber incidents, issuing alerts, and conducting drills.
- **Cyber Swachhta Kendra:** Botnet cleaning and malware analysis center providing free tools to citizens.
- **I4C (Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre):** Tracks online fraud, especially crimes against women and children.
- **National Cyber Security Policy:** Framework for protecting critical infrastructure (power grids, banking systems).

#### 9. Blockchain Technology
- **Definition:** A decentralized, distributed digital ledger where data is stored in cryptographic "blocks" chained together. Once added, data is **immutable** (cannot be altered).
- **Key Properties:**
    - **Decentralization:** No single point of failure; no middleman required.
    - **Immutability:** Data once recorded cannot be changed.
    - **Transparency:** All participants can verify transactions.
- **Consensus Mechanisms:**
    - **Proof of Work (PoW):** Computationally intensive but highly secure. Used by Bitcoin.
    - **Proof of Stake (PoS):** Energy-efficient alternative. Used by Ethereum 2.0.
- **Applications:** Cryptocurrency, supply chain management, healthcare records, digital identity, voting systems.

#### 10. Ethics, Netiquettes & Digital Citizenship
- **Netiquettes (Network + Etiquette):** Rules of conduct for digital communication.
    - Avoid ALL CAPS (considered shouting online).
    - Verify information before forwarding (fight misinformation).
    - Respect others' privacy and intellectual property.
    - Be concise and professional in emails.
    - Do not spam.
- **Intellectual Property Rights (IPR):** Legal rights protecting creators against digital piracy and copyright infringement. Types: Copyright, Patent, Trademark.
- **Digital Citizenship:** The responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology. Includes digital literacy, digital security, and respecting others online.
- **Security vs. Privacy:** Security = protection from unauthorized access/theft; Privacy = individual's right to control their personal data.

#### 11. VPN (Virtual Private Network)
Encrypts the user's internet connection and hides their IP address, ensuring privacy and security on public networks. Acts as a secure tunnel between the user and the internet.
