# Panjab University: Management Information Systems (MIS)
## Important Examination Questions (High-Frequency Clusters)

### Section A: Short Definitions & Concepts
1. **Define MIS, DSS, TPS, and ERP:** Provide precise technical definitions and give one organizational example for each.
2. **Data vs Information:** Distinguish between raw data and processed information. What are the three dimensions (quality, timeliness, relevance) of high-quality information?
3. **System Characteristics:** Define the five primary characteristics of a system: Organization, Interaction, Interdependence, Integration, and Central Objective.
4. **Open vs. Closed Systems:** What is the difference between an Open System and a Closed System? Provide organizational examples illustrating why a purely closed business system faces entropy.
5. **System Classification:** Differentiate between physical systems (hardware) and abstract systems (conceptual models/hierarchies).
6. **System Maintenance:** List and briefly explain the four types of system maintenance (Corrective, Adaptive, Perfective, Preventive).

### Section B & C: Long Answers & Core Models

**7. Herbert Simon's Model of Decision-Making (10 Marks)**
Explain Herbert Simon's Model of Decision-Making in detail. Draw the Intelligence-Design-Choice-Implementation flow diagram. How does the concept of 'Bounded Rationality' and 'Satisficing' contrast with classical economic views of decision-making?

**8. System Development Life Cycle (SDLC)**
Detail the phases of the System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) from planning to maintenance. Emphasize the critical importance of Requirement Analysis and the creation of the Software Requirement Specification (SRS) document. 

**9. Structural Hierarchy of MIS**
Discuss the structural hierarchy of MIS across different management levels. Explain how Transaction Processing Systems (TPS) serve operational staff, Decision Support Systems (DSS) serve middle management, and Executive Information Systems (EIS) serve top executives.

**10. Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs)**
Explain the strict rules for constructing Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs) during system design. What is the difference between a Context Diagram (Level 0) and subsequent lower-level detailed diagrams? Provide an example of how data must flow through processes.

**11. The Role of a Systems Analyst**
What is the strategic role of a Systems Analyst in the development of a new MIS? Discuss how they act as a bridge between business users (stakeholders) and technical developers (programmers), and list their core responsibilities.

**12. Functional MIS Applications**
Organizations rely on integrated functional subsystems. Discuss the specific information needs and outputs of Marketing (Marketing MIS) and Human Resources (HRIS). How do these systems contribute to the overall efficiency of the enterprise?

**13. Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)**
What is Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)? Discuss its fundamental benefits over conventional, siloed software systems. Briefly describe the phases of an ERP implementation lifecycle, including package selection and business process re-engineering (BPR).

**14. Data Warehousing and Business Intelligence**
How does a Data Warehouse differ from a standard Operational Database (TPS)? Explain its architecture and its role alongside Data Mining in generating Business Intelligence (BI) for strategic decision-making.

**15. Competitive Strategic Advantage**
Explain Michael Porter's Competitive Forces Model. How can modern organizations leverage Information Systems to counter the bargaining power of buyers/suppliers, threat of new entrants, and industry rivalry to achieve strategic cost leadership or differentiation?
